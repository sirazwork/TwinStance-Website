How to Send Cold Emails That Actually Get Replies (Without Landing in Spam)

📅 February 2025⏱ 6 min read✍️ TwinStance Team

Every day, millions of cold emails are sent in India. Most of them are opened, scanned for half a second, and deleted. A tiny fraction get a reply. An even tinier fraction close a deal. What separates the ignored email from the one that gets a reply? Three things: the subject line, the personalisation, and the timing.

This is not a theoretical guide. These are the exact principles we use to run campaigns for our clients — and that we use ourselves to grow TwinStance.

Why Most Cold Emails Fail

Before we get into what works, let us talk about what does not. Most cold email campaigns fail for one of these reasons:

  • They sound like spam — "Dear Sir/Madam, I am writing to you regarding our services..."
  • They are about the sender, not the recipient — "We are a 10-year-old company with 500 clients..."
  • They have no clear ask — After reading it, the person has no idea what you want from them
  • They go to the wrong person — Sending to info@ or a generic address that no one reads
  • They land in spam — Technical deliverability issues before the email even reaches the inbox

1. The Subject Line is Everything

Your subject line has one job: get the email opened. That is it. It does not need to explain your whole offer. It just needs to create enough curiosity or relevance that the person clicks.

❌ Avoid This

"Introducing Our Digital Marketing Services for Your Business Growth"

✅ Use This

"Quick question about your website"

❌ Avoid This

"Best SEO Services at Affordable Prices"

✅ Use This

"Found something on your Google ranking"

Short, specific, curiosity-driven subject lines outperform generic ones every time. Keep it under 7 words. Make it feel personal, not promotional.

2. Personalise the First Line

The first sentence of your email determines if they keep reading. A generic opener kills everything. A personalised opener shows you actually know who they are.

❌ Generic

"I hope this email finds you well. I am writing to inform you about our services..."

✅ Personal

"Noticed your restaurant is on Google Maps but does not have a website yet — you are probably losing 30% of the customers who search for you."

This does not need to be elaborate. Even mentioning the person's business type, city, or a specific thing you noticed about them is enough to make the email feel non-automated.

3. Make it About Them, Not You

This is the biggest mistake in cold outreach. Nobody cares how many clients you have, how long you have been in business, or what awards you won. They care about one thing: what is in it for them?

Every sentence in your email should answer the question: "So what does this mean for me?" If it does not, remove it.

💡 A good cold email should make the reader think: "How did they know this was a problem for me?" — not "This person is trying to sell me something."

4. One Clear Call to Action

At the end of your email, ask for one specific, low-commitment thing. Not "buy our package" — that is too much to ask of a stranger. Instead:

  • "Can we get on a 10-minute call this week?"
  • "Would you be open to a free website audit?"
  • "Is this something you are thinking about for the next 3 months?"

One ask. Simple. Easy to say yes to.

5. Timing and Batch Sending

Sending 500 emails at once is the fastest way to get flagged as spam. Email providers like Gmail and Outlook monitor sending patterns. A sudden burst of emails from a new address will go straight to junk.

Instead, send in small batches with delays between each email. Best times to send cold emails in India:

  • Tuesday to Thursday — weekdays in the middle of the week get the most attention
  • 9am to 11am — people are at their desks but not yet overwhelmed
  • Avoid Mondays — inboxes are flooded with weekend catchup
  • Avoid Fridays and weekends — people are in wind-down mode

6. Keep It Short

Cold emails should be 4 to 6 sentences maximum. That is it. If you cannot explain your value in 6 sentences, your offer is not clear enough yet. Most people read email on mobile. Long paragraphs get ignored.

⚡ The 4-sentence cold email formula: 1) Personalised opener, 2) The problem you solve, 3) How you solve it, 4) One simple ask.

7. Fix Your Technical Deliverability

Even perfect emails fail if they land in spam. Before sending any campaign, make sure:

  • Your sending domain has SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records set up
  • You are using a reputable email sending service like Brevo or Mailgun
  • Your email list is clean — remove invalid and bounced addresses
  • Your emails include an unsubscribe link
  • Check your spam score before sending (tools like Mail-Tester.com help)

Putting It All Together

A great cold email is short, personal, and makes a simple ask. It arrives at the right time, lands in the inbox, and makes the reader feel like you actually know their situation. That is the formula.

Is it more work than blasting 1,000 generic emails? Yes. Does it get 10 times more replies? Also yes.
